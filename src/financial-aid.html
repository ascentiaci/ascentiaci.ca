Types of Financial Aid

Grants are typically need-based and may be available through government programs. These do not require repayment. Please check the eligibility criteria and application process for any grants you may be eligible for.

Student loans are a common way to finance your education. Our admissions advisors can guide you through the process of applying for federal and provincial student loans, as well as private loan options. Understand the terms and conditions, and find the best loan options to suit your needs.

We offer flexible payment plans to help you manage the cost of your education. These plans allow you to spread out your tuition payments over the course of your program. Contact our admissions advisors to discuss payment plan options.

Additional Financial Resources

  • WorkBC: Depending on your qualifications, you may be eligible for financial assistance through WorkBC. They offer various programs and services to support your educational and career goals.
  • Employer Training Incentives: Some employers offer training incentives to support their employees' professional development. Check with your employer to see if they provide any financial assistance for further education.

How to Apply for Financial Aid

  1. Research Your Options: Start by exploring the different types of financial aid available. Visit our sections on grants, loans, and payment plans for detailed information.
  2. Complete Your Application: Gather the necessary documents and complete the financial aid application forms. Make sure to meet all deadlines to ensure timely processing.
  3. Submit Your Application: Submit your completed application along with any required documents to our admissions office. Our team will review your application and notify you of your eligibility and award amounts.
  4. Receive Your Aid: Once your application is approved, you will receive your financial aid award letter detailing the types and amounts of aid you will receive.
